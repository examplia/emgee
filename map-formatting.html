Map Formatting

General

  • Maps follow a strict format in order for them to be properly loaded in the game.
  • You will NOT be able to load your map if this format is not PERFECTLY followed.
  • Some folders are not absolutely necessary for your map to load, however it is highly recommended to include every folder in your map, whether it is being used or not.
  • Folders and models can be used interchangeably, so use whichever you prefer when building your map!

Tips

  • Keep a copy of the test map for your map’s gamemode at all times in your workspace! Once you’ve finished the geometry (or Ignore, Doors, etc.), simply replace the test-map parts with your own.
  • Play around on the test maps in-game to discover shoot-through walls, open/close doors, walkable surfaces, and anything else interesting!
  • Follow along with the test maps and prop pack when reading this guide!
  • USE THE TEST MAPS!!!

Geometry

  • The Geometry folder contains most of the physical environment that players see and interact with.
  • Without it, players wouldn't have a map to even play on!
  • All parts in the Geometry folder will block hitscan (bullets and melee).
  • Disabling CanCollide allows projectiles (rockets, grenades, mad milk, etc.) and players to pass through the part.
  • Disabling CanTouch allows stickies to bounce off the part rather than stick to it if CanCollide is enabled.
  • The only parts of maps that are not included in Geometry are player interactables (Doors, kits, KillBricks) and parts that do not block hitscan.

Ignore

  • The Ignore folder contains CameraSpots, KillBricks, NoBuilds, and Water.
  • Parts in Ignore are completely ignored by hitscan and projectiles, excluding Annihilator's projectiles, which behave just as they do in Geometry.
  • CameraSpots contain spectator camera positions—subfolders: General, GRN, RED. A NumberValue “FOV” adjusts field of view.
  • KillBricks instantly kill players on contact.
  • NoBuilds prevent building in their bounds.
  • Water turns parts into terrain water.

Doors

  • The Doors folder contains doors that open/close based on player interaction and capture stages.
  • Door models have two core attributes:
    • Move: studs to move when opened.
    • MoveTime: seconds to move.
  • Door parts behave like Geometry (collisions, projectiles).
  • Each model must contain:
    • PrimaryPart under Pivot—moves when door opens; must be Anchored.
    • Trigger—detects input. “Properties” config:
      • ActivationType (1–4): 1=touch (no collide), 2=melee, 3=projectiles, etc.
      • Duration: how long door stays open.
      • PressMax: uses per round (–1=unlimited).
      • Team: “GRN”, “RED”, or blank.
      • WaitForFinish: wait until close before re-trigger.
    • ForceCloseDuring: stages door cannot be opened (Setup, Stage1–3).
    • ForceOpenDuring: stages door stays permanently open; cannot overlap ForceCloseDuring.

Clips

  • Invisible walls blocking players.
  • Ignored by weapons but block players—CanCollide enabled.

Items

  • Resupply cabinets and health/ammo kits.
  • Models match in-game sizes; use them!
  • Parts named “Resupply” cabinet; “SizeType” for kits (“MediumAmmo”, “SmallHP”).

ControlPoints

  • For KOTH, AD, 3CP, Arena.
  • KOTH/Arena: folder “ControlPoint” with hill.
  • AD: ControlPoint1 then ControlPoint2.
  • 3CP: CP1=Red start, CP2=neutral, CP3=Green start.
  • Contains PlayerCaptureTime, PointLocation, CaptureZone.

Briefcases

  • CTF objectives: “Green” & “Red”.
  • Any part named accordingly.
  • GreenTrigger / RedTrigger fire on opposing carrier touch.

TeamSpecific

  • Folders: GRN & RED with Spawns, Checkpoints, SpawnZones, Barriers.
  • Spawns: respawn points per stage.
  • Checkpoints: optional respawns.
  • SpawnZones: class zones; opposing kills.
  • Barriers: block only opposite team.

Settings

  • Lighting, Terrain, General.
  • Use TC2 Plugin for automation.
  • General toggles (AllowUnanchoredParts, Climbing) off unless needed.

GameMode

  • GameMode sets: "KOTH","AD","3CP","CTF","Arena","None".
  • KOTH: adds ActivateIn IntValue.
  • AD: adds GameTimer, FirstCap, SetupTimer.
  • 3CP: no extra values.
  • CTF: adds CritsOnCap, MaxTime, PlayTo, TimeOnCap.
  • Arena: adds ActivateIn, PrepTime.
  • None: adds PrepTime (infinite timer; end via VIP).
